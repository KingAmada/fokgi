Commitment to Survival

The Nigerian Post-Transplant Reality

A transplant is not the end of treatment. It is the beginning of lifelong survival.

Imagine the profound joy of a successful kidney transplant—the relief of saying goodbye to grueling dialysis. Now, imagine the sheer terror of realizing you cannot afford the daily medication required to keep that new kidney alive.

In Nigeria, the average monthly cost of immunosuppressants exceeds ₦150,000 to ₦300,000. Without robust health insurance coverage, families are forced to sell assets, children drop out of school, and eventually, financial fatigue sets in.

The tragic result? Highly preventable graft loss and death simply because a patient ran out of money for pills.

The Medication Saves the Life.

Our Intercept: The ARDS Initiative

FOKGI’s Anti-Rejection Drug Scheme (ARDS) provides structured financial assistance to indigent patients, ensuring they never have to choose between feeding their families and keeping their transplanted organ functioning.

Who We Help

Paediatric & Adolescent Patients

Ensuring children can return to school and live normal lives post-transplant.

Indigent & Low-Income Earners

Patients facing catastrophic medical debt and at high risk of medication default.

Financially Fatigued Families

Families who have sold assets to afford the surgery and are now struggling with maintenance.

How Support Works

1

Application & Referral

Patients are referred by partner nephrologists, hospitals, or apply directly for ARDS support.

2

Medical & Financial Verification

Our advisory board reviews clinical history and conducts socioeconomic verification.

3

Subsidy Approval

Approved patients receive a structured subsidy plan for specific immunosuppressants.

4

Pharmacy/Hospital Support & Follow-up

Funds are directed to partner pharmacies/hospitals to guarantee patients receive authentic medication, paired with regular clinical follow-up.

About FOKGI

Foundation of Kidney Transplant Global Initiative

A non-governmental, non-political, and not-for-profit organisation bridging the gap in post-transplant care across Nigeria and the world.

Dedicated to Giving a Second Chance at Life

Our Vision

A world in which no person is denied a healthy life because of kidney failure.

We envision a society where every transplant recipient—whether adult or child—has reliable, uninterrupted access to the care they need to thrive, return to work, and live fully without the catastrophic burden of medical debt.

Our Mission

To improve outcomes for kidney patients by expanding access to affordable care.

We support research, raise public awareness on ethical organ donation, and fiercely advocate for the integration of transplant services into national and state health insurance benefit packages.

Our Strategic Objectives

Anti-Rejection Subsidies

Providing partial subsidies and structured assistance toward the cost of immunosuppressants for indigent patients to prevent graft loss.

Paediatric Protection

Under our Director for Children and Young Transplant Persons, we provide age-appropriate clinical support, school re-integration, and child safeguarding.

Ethical Organ Donation

Promoting voluntary living and deceased organ donation. Addressing cultural barriers while strictly opposing organ trafficking.
